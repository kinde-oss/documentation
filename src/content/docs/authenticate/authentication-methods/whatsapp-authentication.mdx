You can send one-time passcodes (OTP) and multi-factor authentication (MFA) codes via **WhatsApp** in addition to SMS. When WhatsApp is set up and enabled, Kinde prefers WhatsApp for delivering codes and falls back to SMS if WhatsApp delivery fails.

<Aside>

**Prerequisites:** You must have a **verified WhatsApp Business account**. Messaging is billed by Meta; Kinde does not charge for WhatsApp delivery. You use your own Meta account and credentials.

</Aside>

## Navigate to WhatsApp settings

All messaging options (Email, SMS, and WhatsApp) are under a single section in Kinde:

1. In Kinde, go to **Settings > Environment > Messaging**.
2. Open the **WhatsApp** page.

## Obtain WhatsApp credentials from Meta

You need two values from Meta’s WhatsApp Manager:

- **WhatsApp API token** (sometimes called “access token”)
- **WhatsApp Business Account ID**

To get these:

1. Sign in to your [Meta Business Suite](https://business.facebook.com/) (or Meta Business Manager).
2. Go to **WhatsApp Manager** (or **WhatsApp** in the left menu).
3. Create or select your WhatsApp Business Account and complete any verification Meta requires.
4. In WhatsApp Manager, find your **Business Account ID** and create or copy an **API token** for the WhatsApp Business API.

Meta’s exact menu names can vary. If you don’t see these options, check [Meta’s WhatsApp Business API documentation](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/whatsapp/cloud-api/get-started) for the current steps.

## Configure WhatsApp in Kinde

1. In Kinde, go to **Settings > Environment > Messaging > WhatsApp**.
2. Enable **Enable WhatsApp** to show the configuration options.
3. Enter your **WhatsApp token** (API token from Meta).
4. Enter your **WhatsApp Business Account ID**.
5. (Optional) Enter a **Template name** if you want to use a specific WhatsApp message template. If left blank, Kinde uses a default template.
6. Select **Sync WhatsApp** to sync with the WhatsApp service. After syncing, the **WhatsApp phone number** dropdown appears.
7. In **WhatsApp phone number**, select the phone number you want to use for sending codes.
8. Select **Save**.

Once saved, OTP and MFA codes will be sent via WhatsApp when possible, with fallback to SMS if WhatsApp fails.

## Language and WhatsApp messages

- The language used for WhatsApp messages is determined by **Settings > Environment > Languages**. Choose the languages your users need.
- If a selected language is **not supported by WhatsApp**, Kinde uses **en-US** for that case.
- After you **change the language** settings, run **Sync WhatsApp** again so the updated languages apply to your WhatsApp templates.

## Pricing and billing

- **Kinde does not charge** for sending messages via WhatsApp.
- **Meta charges** for WhatsApp Business messaging according to [Meta’s pricing](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/whatsapp/pricing). You pay Meta using your own Meta/WhatsApp Business account.

## Troubleshooting

### Templates fail to create or sync

If template creation or syncing fails in Kinde, the cause is often on the **Meta/WhatsApp side**. Check the following:

1. **Verify your WhatsApp Business account** – Ensure the business is verified in Meta and that you can create and use templates in [WhatsApp Manager](https://business.facebook.com/) (or the equivalent Meta tool). If you can create a template manually there, your business setup is usually correct.
2. **Token and Business Account ID** – Confirm the WhatsApp API token and Business Account ID in Kinde match the values in your Meta account and that the token has the right permissions.
3. **Sync again** – After fixing credentials or templates in Meta, run **Sync WhatsApp** again in Kinde.

### Messages not received by users

- Confirm the user’s phone number is in a format supported by WhatsApp (e.g. E.164 with country code).
- Ensure WhatsApp is enabled for the user’s region and that they have WhatsApp installed and active.
- If WhatsApp delivery fails, Kinde will fall back to SMS when SMS is configured; check that [SMS is set up](/authenticate/authentication-methods/phone-authentication/) under **Settings > Environment > Messaging > SMS** if you rely on fallback.

### After changing languages

Whenever you change **Settings > Environment > Languages**, run **Sync WhatsApp** again so your WhatsApp message templates use the updated languages (or en-US where a language isn’t supported by WhatsApp).
